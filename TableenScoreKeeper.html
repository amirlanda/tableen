Rules

Number of Players: 2
Starting the Game:

Whichever player is better at cheating deals first.

Starting a Round:

The dealer deals 4 cards to the other player and 4 cards to him/herself.

If the other player wants to keep his/her cards, the dealer deals 4 community cards face up.

If the other player does not want to keep his/her cards, s/he lays them face up and they become the community cards. The dealer then deals 4 new cards to the other player.

Playing a Hand:

The player who is not dealing goes first.

Players take turns playing one of the cards in their hands to collect community cards that are equal to the value of the card played (e.g. 10 can collect a 10, 6 and 4, or 7 and 3).

If a player clears the table by collecting all of the available community cards in a turn, s/he marks this by turning a card face down (see Cleared Tables picture).

If a player is unable to collect any community cards, or there are no community cards, the player must add one card from their hand to the community cards.

NOTE: If a player can only play a Jack and there are no community cards, the Jack becomes a community card with a value of 11. The player does not clear the table.

After both players have used all of the cards in their hands, the dealer deals 4 new cards to each player. No new community cars are added.

Ending a Round:

The dealer continues dealing hands until all of the cards in the deck have been played.

Any remaining community cards go to the last person to collect a community card.

Calculate how many points each player earned in the round.

NOTE: If each player has an equal number of cards, they each get one point.

Switch the dealer for the next round.

Winning the Game:

The first player to earn 31 points wins.

A player does not have to wait until the end of a round to win. S/he can win in the middle of a round, and even the middle of a hand.

For example, a player has 30 points at the end of a round. If s/he collect an ace or clear the table in the first hand of the next round, theyâ€™ve won!

For this reason, rounds toward the end of the game are played point-by-point.
